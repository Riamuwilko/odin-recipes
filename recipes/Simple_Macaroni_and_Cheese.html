Simple Macaroni and Cheese

Description

Macaroni and cheese, often referred to as mac and cheese, is a classic comfort dish made by combining tender elbow macaroni pasta with a rich, creamy cheese sauce. The sauce is typically made from a blend of melted cheeses, butter, and milk, creating a smooth, indulgent texture that coats the pasta. Some recipes add a crispy topping, like breadcrumbs or extra cheese, and bake it until golden brown. Mac and cheese is beloved for its simple yet satisfying combination of flavors and textures, offering a warm, hearty meal that's perfect on its own or as a side dish.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz elbow macaroni
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese (or cheese blend)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: 1/2 cup breadcrumbs for topping
  • Optional: Extra grated cheese for topping

    • Steps

    1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and cook the elbow macaroni according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
    2. Make the Cheese Sauce: In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for about 1 minute to create a roux. Gradually add the milk, whisking continuously until the mixture thickens.
    3. Add the Cheese: Stir in the shredded cheese, salt, and pepper, and cook until the cheese is fully melted and the sauce is smooth.
    4. Combine and Serve: Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and stir until fully coated. Optionally, transfer to a baking dish, top with extra cheese or breadcrumbs, and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 15-20 minutes until golden.
